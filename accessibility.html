Accessibility Statement

Our commitment to inclusive design and equal access for all users

Last updated: March 20, 2026

1. Our Commitment

Socia Visual is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people of all abilities. We continually improve the user experience for everyone and apply the relevant accessibility standards to ensure we provide equal access to all users.

2. Conformance Status

We strive to conform to the following accessibility standards:

3. Accessibility Features

sociavisual.com includes the following accessibility features:

3.1 Navigation and Structure

  • Skip-to-content links on every page
  • Semantic HTML5 landmarks (header, main, nav, footer)
  • Logical heading hierarchy (h1 through h3)
  • ARIA labels and roles for interactive components
  • Keyboard-navigable interface throughout the site

3.2 Visual Design

  • Color contrast ratios meeting WCAG AA standards (minimum 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)
  • No content conveyed by color alone
  • Visible focus indicators on all interactive elements
  • Support for prefers-reduced-motion — all animations and transitions are disabled when this preference is set
  • Support for prefers-color-scheme via color-scheme meta tag

3.3 Interactive Content

  • WAI-ARIA radiogroup pattern with roving tabindex for theme selectors
  • Modal dialogs with focus trapping and Escape key dismissal
  • Live regions (aria-live) for dynamic content announcements
  • Form inputs with associated labels and error descriptions
  • Decorative images marked with aria-hidden="true"

3.4 Alternative Content

  • Text alternatives for all non-text content
  • Screen-reader-only descriptions for 3D/WebGL experiences
  • Descriptive aria-label attributes on canvas elements
  • <noscript> fallbacks for JavaScript-dependent content
  • External links indicate when they open in a new tab (screen reader text)

4. Technologies Used

Accessibility of sociavisual.com relies on the following technologies:

  • HTML5
  • CSS3
  • JavaScript (ES6+)
  • WAI-ARIA 1.2
  • Three.js (WebGL, with accessible fallbacks)

These technologies are relied upon for conformance with the accessibility standards used.

5. Known Limitations

While we strive for full accessibility, some limitations exist:

  • 3D Interactive Experiences (games.html): The WebGL-based 3D scenes require mouse interaction and are not fully operable via keyboard alone. Screen-reader descriptions of all scenes are provided as an alternative.
  • Third-party content: Some embedded third-party services (Google reCAPTCHA) may have their own accessibility limitations outside our control.

6. Feedback

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of sociavisual.com. If you encounter accessibility barriers or have suggestions for improvement, please contact us:

Email: scott@sociavisual.com

We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within 5 business days and to resolve reported issues within 30 days.

7. Enforcement

If you are not satisfied with our response to your accessibility concern, you may escalate the matter to the relevant regulatory authority in your jurisdiction. In the United States, this may include filing a complaint with the U.S. Department of Justice (opens in new tab) or the U.S. Access Board (opens in new tab).

8. Assessment Approach

Socia Visual assessed the accessibility of sociavisual.com through the following methods:

  • Manual code review against WCAG 2.1 Level AA success criteria
  • Keyboard-only navigation testing
  • Screen reader compatibility review
  • Color contrast ratio verification
  • Section 508 compliance audit (subsections 1194.22 a–p)
  • ADA Title III effective communication review
  • GDPR data protection and consent mechanism audit
